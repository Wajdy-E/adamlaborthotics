Frequently Asked Questions

Custom-made orthotics are specially designed insoles that fit the unique contours of your feet, providing comfort and support tailored to your specific needs.

If you experience foot, heel, or lower back pain, custom-made orthotics may help. Consult with a healthcare professional to determine if orthotics are right for you.

Custom-made orthotics typically last between 2 to 3 years, depending on usage, materials, and how well they are maintained.

In most cases, you will need a prescription from a healthcare provider, such as a podiatrist or orthopedic specialist, to obtain custom-made orthotics. This ensures that the orthotics are tailored to your specific needs.

Custom-made orthotics can be designed to fit a wide range of footwear, including athletic shoes, dress shoes, and even boots. However, some shoe styles, such as high heels, may require specialized orthotics.

Custom-made orthotics are crafted using a detailed mold or scan of your feet. The process involves taking a plaster cast, foam impression, or digital scan of your feet to create orthotics that are perfectly tailored to your foot structure and gait.

Many insurance plans offer coverage for custom-made orthotics, but coverage can vary depending on your specific policy and provider. It's important to check with your insurance company to understand the extent of your coverage and any necessary documentation or referrals required for reimbursement.
